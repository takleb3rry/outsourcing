## Project Context

We are scoring professional credentialing organizations as sourcing partners for recruiting ~100 domain experts to review AI-generated workflow outputs for Anthropic. The goal is to identify which organizations to approach for partnerships that give us access to qualified, credentialed practitioners across the workflow clusters listed below.

Channel 1 ("The Rolodex Raid") is the primary sourcing strategy. Other channels (freelance platforms, community posts, referral cascades) are supporting. The credential body partnerships ARE the pitch — they signal infrastructure, quality, and seriousness to Anthropic.

| Priority | Weight | Rationale |

## Cross-Cluster Rules

These apply to every scoring run regardless of cluster:

1. **No double-counting adjacent organizations.** If a parent body and its sub-chapter both appear (e.g., CFA Institute globally and CFA Society South Africa), score the parent body only unless the local chapter provides materially different access or reach.

2. **Score current state, not aspirational state.** "They're planning to expand to Africa" scores zero on geographic reach today.

3. **Domain fit is scored per workflow cluster, not globally.** The CFA Institute might score 9/10 for IB/Valuation and 3/10 for Insurance. Same body, different score depending on which cluster it appears in.

## University Consideration

University partners are secondary to credential body partnerships. 1–2 university deals max, leveraging existing personal relationships. Universities are slow. Credential bodies are the primary channel.

**Threshold:** Do not include a university unless it is THE undisputed #1 global name in a narrow specialty directly relevant to the cluster (e.g., Temple for Actuarial Science, Georgia State for Risk & Insurance). "Generally prestigious" does not meet this bar.

**UMass system specifically:** Only a candidate if demonstrably top-tier in a specific domain. If not, including them invites the "Why not Harvard?" objection — which we can't answer. The threshold is the same as any other university: undisputed #1 in a narrow relevant specialty, or exclude.
